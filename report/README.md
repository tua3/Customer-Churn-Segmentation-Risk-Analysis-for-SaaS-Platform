## Objective
Identify behavioral and demographic factors driving churn among paid SaaS users to improve retention and revenue stability.

## Key Findings
- Mobile users show higher churn compared to desktop users.
- Paid acquisition channels have higher churn than organic/referral.
- Users who publish quickly after signing up are significantly less likely to churn.

## Recommendations
- Improve mobile product usability and onboarding.
- Prioritize organic and referral growth channels.
- Trigger early engagement campaigns for slow-activation users.
- Flag high-risk accounts for proactive customer success outreach.

## Expected Impact
Reducing early-stage churn will increase lifetime value (LTV), improve revenue predictability, and reduce acquisition dependency.
